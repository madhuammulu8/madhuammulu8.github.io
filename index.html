8 Puzzle Solver using Search Algorithms:

Project Description :This Project explores Uniform Cost Search, and the A Star algorithm with Misplaced Tile Heuristic and the Manhattan Distance Heuristic. There were puzzles of varying difficulty given to implement. The easiest one being the goal state itself and the hardest one being impossible to solve. However, not much difference was found for the easier puzzles. But as the difficulty level increased, the heuristics performed better with respect to time and space complexity. It was found out from the comparison of the three algorithms that A Star with Manhattan Distance Heuristic performed the best, followed by Misplaced Tile Heuristic, followed by Uniform Cost Search. Though both the heuristics performed better than Uniform Cost Search(heuristic hardcoded to 0), the Manhattan Distance Heuristic performed the better. The language used to develop the algorithms

Automatic Solar Panel Cleaning Robot Using Firebird V Microcontroller:

Project Description : When it came to working on projects, I chose to focus on my primary objective, i.e. to utilize my understanding of the field in furthering the use of renewable energy sources. Working on developing a solar panel cleaning robot using firebird V was my final year project that was aimed at increasing the efficiency of solar panels. Through the project, I developed a rotating cylindrical brush made of microfiber to enable dry cleaning as most solar panel fields are located in areas with water scarcity like deserts. Moreover, working alongside experienced professors on enhancing the efficiency of solar panels present in several college blocks was a unique experience that gave me the opportunity to contribute to real-world systems. I published the results of the project as a research paper titled ‘Automatic Solar panel cleaning robot using Firebird V’ in the IJESC Publications.
